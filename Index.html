About Me

Born and raised in Hong Kong, I am someone who is familiar with many different languages and cultures. Inspired by video games since young, now I aim to give back to the community by creating the very things that helped inspire me in my youth. As an aspiring game developer and 3D modeler, my adaptability allows me to work with both AGILE and Waterfall methodologies, and my problem solving skills and desire for self-improvement make me a crucial part of any team.

More About Me

One of my earliest memories was playing the original Lego Creator game on a Kindergarden pc. Back then, I would revel in the infinite possiblities and endless combinations of builds that could be made just by rearranging the same pieces in different places. To me, Coding is an art form that is very much similar, allowing me to create individual parts that function individually but only really come to life when they are combined to form the final product.

While the code is the framework that holds everything together, UI and graphics are what the consumer ultimately sees. As someone who is heavily inspired by Gundam and other mecha anime/games, I also enjoy creating and animating 3D models of mechs and general sci-fi goodness in my spare time.

Some of my Recent Projects

Chron

Chron is a 2D platformer made using FXGL, a Java/JavaFX/Kotin Game Library Engine which uses a superset of Java. Built from the ground up for Windows/Mac/Linux/Android/IOS devices, Chron uses integrated Jbox2D system for physics, movement and collisions, allowing for both fluid and rigid-body simulations in real time.

Acolyte

Acolyte is made a game in the Unity game engine with C# over the period of 72 hours for the 43rd Ludum Dare game jam, under the theme of “Sacrifices must be made.” It is a A 2D puzzle game with procedurally generated quests and minigames where the objective is to discover and perform the correct ritual to bring about the apocalypse.

Turtle's Bizzare Adventure

Turtle's Bizzare Adventure is a side scrolling horizontal runner style game developed with the unity engine using C#, featuring a turtle attempting to return to their beach while avoiding hazards that humans have dumped into the sea. The game uses Procedurally generated obstacles which help to ensure game replayability and a time based scoring system which encourages player retention through competition. The game was developed over the period of 72 hours for the 2019 Ukie Green Game Jam under the themes of climate change and sustainability.
